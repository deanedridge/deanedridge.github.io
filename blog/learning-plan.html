Learning Plan

  • What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?
    • To become a professional Web Developer — preferrably working for someone else in a dev studio somewhere in Auckland.
  • A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning
    • I can at times spend too much time on a problem before moving on to something else or getting help with the problem.
  • What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?
    • To learn to interact with other students more. Increase my confidence. Increase my communication skills. And to improve my ability to manage my time better. Improve motivation levels.
  • A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:
    • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives
      • I promise that I will do my very best to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives. I will achieve this by being present online a decent amount of hours each day being available and ready to communicate with the necessary people. I will also respond to all messages in a timely manner and always be willing to communicate with these people. I promise to be polite and courtious at all times when communicating with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives
    • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you
      • If things build up and start to get to me I will reach out to my support worker for guidance and general help. I will also reach out to my facilitator for guidance on what is best to do. I will be open and honest with these people and work productively with them.
  • A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way
    • If things build up and start to get to me I promise I will reach out to my support worker, who is a family member, for guidance and general support and help. I will also reach out to my facilitator for guidance on what is best to do and keep them informed. I will be open and honest with these people and work productively with them. I will do all this in a timely manner and always keep the communication pathways open
  • A description of what you expect from the facilitation team
    • I expect the facilitation team to respond to me within 24 hours of a working day to questions or help requests I might have. It would also be good if they encouraged me also.
  • Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It’s important to plan these in.
    • I promise to communicate with my facilitator and keep them informed to any appointments I might have or for any reason I might not be available or away from my computer for a long period of time.

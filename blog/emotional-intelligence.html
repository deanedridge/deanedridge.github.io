Emotional Intelligence and Intelligence Quotient

    • What is Emotional Intelligence?
        1. Being able to identify emotions
        2. Being able to relate to others
        3. Being able to communicate socially
        Emotional Intelligence, (or emotional intelligence quotient) EQ, sometimes called "Street Smarts" refers to the ability to perceive, control, and evaluate emotions; and it is said that it is important in determining one's success in life.
    • How is it different to IQ?
      • IQ represents abilities such as:
        • Visual and spatial processing
        • Knowledge of the world
        • Fluid reasoning
        • Working memory and short-term memory
        • Quantitative reasoning
      • "Emotional intelligence refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions" - https://www.verywellmind.com/iq-or-eq-which-one-is-more-important-2795287. It also means the ability to:
        • Identify emotions
        • Relate to others
        • Evaluating or perceive how others feel
        • Controlling one's own emotions
        • Use emotions to facilitate social communication
    • Why is it important?
      • Emotional Intelligence is important because in some professions a job candidate might be tested on their EQ when applying fo a job. EQ is also important in the sales prefession as, "Sales agents who ranked lower on emotional intelligence abilities such as empathy, initiative, and self-confidence were found to sell policies with an average premium of $54,000. [Whereas a]gents who ranked highly on measures of EQ sold policies worth an average of $114,000." - https://www.verywellmind.com/iq-or-eq-which-one-is-more-important-2795287
